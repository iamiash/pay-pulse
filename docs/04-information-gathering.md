To figure out what features PayPulse actually needs, I researched existing budgeting tools and looked at how everyday students track their tech expenses.

Key Insights Found:
Most students avoid using traditional budgeting apps because those apps require linking real bank accounts, which feels invasive and risky for a simple utility project.
The bare minimum details people actually care about tracking are: the Service Name, the Price, the Renewal Date, and the Cycle (Monthly vs. Yearly).
Visual simplicity is key. If it takes more than 30 seconds to log a subscription, users will stop updating the app entirely.
